HISTORY

Amarei Sari-Sari Store started its journey on January 8,2021, with a simple goal of serving the community with affordable and quality products. From the very first day, the store has aimed to be a friendly and reliable place where customers feel welcome and valued. With the support of the people around and a passion for providing good service, the store continues to improve and remain a part of everyday life in the neighborhood.
THE LIFE OF THE OWNER

Mae Teresa Impas is a dedicated sari-sari store owner whose humble business has become the heart of her neighborhood at Arbor, Boljoon, Cebu. Every morning, she opens her small store with a warm smile, offering essentials, snacks, and a familiar sense of comfort to the community. Behind her is a supportive family—her husband, a skilled chef who helps with budgeting and creative food ideas for the store, and their two sons who often assist with arranging goods or entertaining customers. Mae’s life is a blend of hard work and love; she manages inventory, handles customers, and still finds time to prepare meals and care for her family. Her sari-sari store not only helps sustain their daily needs but also brings people together, proving that even a small business can create a big impact when run with heart and dedication.
THE SUCCESS STORY OF THE BUSINESS

Amarei Sari-sari Store, owned by Mae Teresa Impas, began as a simple dream—a small corner stall selling basic products and mobile load to support her family. With determination, patience, and the constant support of her chef-husband and two sons, Mae slowly grew her business from a modest setup into a trusted neighborhood store. As her customers’ needs expanded, she expanded too: she began selling rice by the sack, attracting more buyers and earning steady daily income. Eventually, Mae introduced printing services, turning her once-tiny store into a multi-service hub for the community. Today, Amarei Sari-sari Store stands as a testament to Mae’s hard work and perseverance—proof that with dedication and smart thinking, a small start can grow into a thriving, reliable business that serves the community in many meaningful ways.
THE CHALLENGES MET BY THE FOUNDER

The journey of Mae Teresa Impas in building Amarei Sari-sari Store was far from easy. When she first began with only a few products and mobile load, one of her biggest challenges was limited capital, which made it difficult to keep up with customer demand and restock regularly. Competition from bigger stores also tested her patience, forcing her to find ways to offer better service and build strong trust within the community. As her store grew and she started selling rice in sacks, Mae experienced the challenge of managing larger inventory, dealing with fluctuating rice prices, and ensuring that every sack was of good quality. Expanding into printing services came with its own obstacles as well—learning the equipment, handling technical issues, and meeting the expectations of customers who relied on her for schoolwork and documents. Despite these hardships, Mae’s determination, adaptability, and family support helped her overcome every difficulty, turning each challenge into a stepping stone toward the success of Amarei Sari-sari Store.
YOUR BUSINESS 10 YEARS AFTER

Ten years from now, I see Amarei Sari-sari Store growing into a more stable and well-known family business in our community. I hope to expand it into a larger store that offers more products, reliable printing and digital services, and maybe even small food items prepared with the help of my husband. I want it to be a business that not only supports my family but also provides opportunities for my sons to learn responsibility and entrepreneurship. Most of all, I see my business continuing to serve people with honesty, quality, and care—remaining a trusted place that grows alongside the community.”
HOW DID THE PHILIPPINE GOVERNMENT HELPED THE BUSINESS GROW

The Philippine government helped businesses grow by providing support such as training, financial assistance, and market access, especially for small enterprises. Through agencies like the Department of Trade and Industry (DTI), entrepreneurs receive free seminars, mentoring, and help in improving their operations. The government also offers loan programs and opportunities to join trade fairs and online platforms, making it easier for small businesses to expand and reach more customers.
